About Me

In college, I wanted to understand how people think and function, so I studied Cognitive Science and Neurobiology. While studying abroad in Argentina, I discovered a love of travel and other cultures, and realized how many doors English language skills open for people worldwide. So I devoted my next three years toward helping international students navigate the US immigration system, and developing excellent learning experiences for them in Boston. During this time, I witnessed how transformational technology can be toward expanding access to services, increasing organizational effectiveness, and genuinely improving lives in countless ways. I decided it was time to pursue a career in Web & Software Development.

To this end, I enrolled in Bloc’s Software Engineering program, where I’ve studied computer science and full-stack web development. I’ve had the opportunity to complete a variety of curricular, personal, and freelance web projects, all under the guidance of experienced industry mentors. I’ve enjoyed this major period of learning, and look forward to growing and advancing as a developer.

When I’m not developing, you can find me reading, hiking, teaching English classes at the library, practicing yoga, or traveling anywhere I can manage. So far, I’ve explored Australia, New Zealand, Argentina, Uruguay, Israel, and Morocco, with many more locations on my list! Please feel free to get in touch with me if you’d like to chat.
